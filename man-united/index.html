01 — Club History

From Newton Heath
to Old Trafford

Six defining eras that shaped one of football's greatest clubs.

1878 — 1945
Newton Heath & The Early Years

Founded as Newton Heath LYR FC by railway workers in 1878. Renamed Manchester United in 1902 after financial crisis. Survived near-bankruptcy, won their first league titles and FA Cups. Matt Busby arrived as manager in 1945, beginning a transformation.

2 League Titles 1 FA Cup
1945 — 1969
The Busby Babes & Munich

Busby built one of England's greatest ever sides. On 6 February 1958 the Munich air disaster killed eight first-team players. Busby survived but was given last rites. He rebuilt — a decade later, with Best, Law and Charlton, United became the first English club to lift the European Cup.

5 League Titles 1 European Cup 2 FA Cups
1969 — 1986
Post-Busby Struggles

Seven managers in seventeen years. United were relegated to the Second Division in 1974 under Tommy Docherty — their lowest moment in decades. Ron Atkinson brought FA Cup wins but no league title. The club was drifting when a Scotsman arrived from Aberdeen.

2 FA Cups
1986 — 2013
The Ferguson Dynasty

Sir Alex Ferguson's 26-year reign is the definitive chapter. Thirteen Premier League titles. The 1999 Treble — league, FA Cup and Champions League in one season — remains English football's greatest achievement. Cantona, Beckham, Keane, Scholes, Giggs, Ronaldo, Rooney. An unmatched legacy.

13 PL Titles 2 European Cups 5 FA Cups 4 League Cups
2013 — 2024
The Post-Ferguson Era

Moyes, Van Gaal, Mourinho, Solskjær, Rangnick, Ten Hag — six permanent managers in eleven years. Moments of silverware punctuated persistent decline. The 2017 Europa League and 2016 & 2024 FA Cups provided relief, but the league title proved elusive as the club searched for identity.

1 Europa League 2 FA Cups 1 League Cup
2024 — Present
The INEOS Era

Sir Jim Ratcliffe's INEOS acquired a 29% stake and took sporting control in 2024. Amorim replaced Ten Hag but lasted fewer than 14 months. Darren Fletcher took interim charge before Michael Carrick — a United legend — was appointed in January 2026. United sit 3rd, in Champions League contention.

3rd — PL 2025/26 CL contention
